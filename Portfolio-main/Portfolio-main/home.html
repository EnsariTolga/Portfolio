Dr. Tolga Ensari is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science in the Department of Engineering and Computing Sciences at Arkansas Tech University, Russellville, Arkansas. He teaches a range of courses in computer science, machine learning, and data science. He earned his Ph.D. in Computer Science from Istanbul University, Turkey, in 2009. He was a visiting scholar at the University of Louisville, Kentucky, in 2012, 2014, and 2019.


Dr. Ensari’s research interests include machine learning, deep learning, and neuromorphic computing. He has 25+ years of research experience in these areas and has authored 100+ scientific journal and conference papers as well as book chapters, with numerous citations in the literature.


He founded a Computational Intelligence and Machine Learning (CIML) Research Lab and has supervised many master’s and Ph.D. theses. He has led and contributed to numerous scientific research projects in machine learning and has delivered many keynote speeches and invited talks in both academia and industry. He has been recognized as a successful international researcher. In 2023, he co-founded the Neuromorphic Computing Research Society to promote interdisciplinary research in brain-inspired and cortical computing and to foster a global community focused on next-generation computing architectures.


Dr. Ensari has received research funding as a Principal Investigator (PI), Co-Principal Investigator (Co-PI), or researcher from organizations including the National Science Foundation (NSF), the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), Arkansas Tech University (ATU), the ITEA4 Program, the Scientific and Technological Research Council of Turkey (TUBITAK), Istanbul University-Cerrahpasa, and the Council of Higher Education.


In addition, he has served as a scientific advisor on research and development (R&D) projects for fourteen commercial companies in industry. Dr. Ensari has contributed to the academic community as a conference general chair, steering committee member, technical program committee member, reviewer, guest editor, and committee member for numerous prestigious journals and conferences, including Nature, IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems, Neurocomputing, Cambridge University Press, Neural Computing and Applications, PLOS, and Current Bioinformatics. He is a member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), the Neuromorphic Computing Research Society, the Hyperdimensional Computing/Vector Symbolic Architectures (HC/VSA) Society, and holds patents.
