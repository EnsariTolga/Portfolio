Computational Intelligence and Machine Learning (CIML) Research Lab:

Welcome to the Computational Intelligence and Machine Learning (CIML) Lab! Our mission is to explore, analyze, and develop innovative systems and architectures based on machine learning, artificial intelligence (AI), and neuromorphic computing.

The CIML Lab is home to a wide range of scientific activities. We conduct research projects, publish scholarly articles, host monthly research seminars, and supervise PhD, Master’s degree, and undergraduate theses. Our team also teaches a variety of courses, contributes books and book chapters, files patent applications, and organizes scientific conferences and workshops. Currently, 11 graduate, undergraduate students and researchers are actively working in the lab, and we are proud to have had 58 former members who contributed to our research community.

Our research focuses on the algorithmic and theoretical foundations of machine learning, AI, and neuromorphic computing. Key areas include deep learning, brain inspired computing, in memory computing, cortical computing, and pattern recognition with applications in several application domains like cancer research, bioinformatics, computer vision, natural language processing, stability and convergence analysis, decision support systems, recommender systems, and other specialized topics.

Our research has been supported by the National Science Foundation (NSF), the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), Arkansas Tech University (ATU), the ITEA4 Program, the Scientific and Technological Research Council of Turkey (TUBITAK), Istanbul University-Cerrahpasa, Istanbul Technical University (ITU), and the Council of Higher Education.

We actively collaborate across disciplines and collaborate with various colleges and departments, including medicine, sociology, and finance. Interdisciplinary collaboration is central to our mission and a key driver of our research success.

For more information, please contact us at tensari@atu.edu

We look forward to welcoming you to the CIML Lab!
